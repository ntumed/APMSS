AI in medicine study guide

Te-Wei Shieh, NTU MED

The most important goal of higher education was to ensure that graduates can recognize when "someone is talking rot". - Jeremy Knowles, former dean of the Faculty of Arts and Sciences, Harvard University.

Artificial intelligence is a surging field in computer science and is said to revolutionize medicine in the near future. However, this AI hype and its unrealistic fantasies on media could be misleading. As medical students, knowing the frontiers and limitations of current artificial intelligence research is crucial for establishing a correct understanding of this field and creating novel medical AI applications.

Main Ref. Eric Topol. High-performance medicine: the convergence of human and artificial intelligence. Nature Medicine volume 25, pages 44–56 (2019).

Goal

Know what could be done by current state-of-the-art machine learning models.
Be able to analyze and design a reasonable medical AI solution.
(Experiences in machine learning, data science or biostatistics are preferred but not required.)

Introduction (15 min)

  • Self Introduction
  • Machine Learning and Information Retrieval
  • Computer Vision / Natural Language Processing
  • Reinforcement Learning and Unsupervised Learning
  • Current AI applications in medicine
  • Some Challenges in medical AI

Discussion (total 105 min)

(in groups of 3~4 people)

Analyze a medical AI application (Select one medical AI paper onsite) (30 min)

  • What’s the objective of this AI application?
  • Describe the dataset, including size, dimensions, endpoints, etc.
  • Describe the authors’ approach: which algorithm did they use and why?
  • Describe the evaluation method and results.

Papers to discuss:

  • Lecouat et al. Semi-Supervised Deep Learning for Abnormality Classification in Retinal Images. ML4H Workshop, NIPS 2018.
  • Hannun et al. Cardiologist-level arrhythmia detection and classification in ambulatory electrocardiograms using a deep neural network. Nature Medicine 2019. project site
  • Ribeiro et al. Automatic Diagnosis of Short-Duration 12-Lead ECG using a Deep Convolutional Network. ML4H Workshop, NIPS 2018.
  • Komorowski et al. The Artificial Intelligence Clinician learns optimal treatment strategies for sepsis in intensive care. Nature Medicine 2018. related work 1, related work 2

Design a medical AI application (40 min)

  • What’s the objective of this AI application?
  • Where is the data? How to collect the desired data?
  • What kind of model will you choose and why?
  • What is the evaluation criteria for your model?
